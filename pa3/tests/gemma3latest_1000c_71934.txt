**Instructions:**

2.  **Infuse the Flavor:** If using a vanilla bean, add the split bean and seeds to the warm cream. Cover and let steep for 15-20 minutes. If using vanilla extract, add it after removing from heat.
3.  **Temper the Egg Yolks:** In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks. Gradually whisk a small amount (about 1/4 cup) of the warm cream into the egg yolks, whisking constantly to temper them and prevent them from scrambling.
4.  **Combine and Strain:** Pour the tempered egg yolk mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining cream. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly with a rubber spatula, until the mixture thickens slightly – it should coat the back of a spoon. *Do not boil!* Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl.
5.  **Add Butter:** Stir in the butter until it is completely melted and incorporated.
6.  **Chill:** Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, pressing the wrap directly onto the surface of the cream to prevent a skin from forming. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, until completely chilled and set.

**Notes:**

*   Temperature is crucial. The cream must be warm enough to cook the egg yolks, but not so hot that they curdle.
*   Don't overcook the cream mixture – it will curdle.
*   Serve chilled. Bavarian Cream is often used as a filling for cakes and pastries.
